Smoked Brisket Recipe (Hot and Fast Method) on Charcoal Grill

I. Ingredients
  • ~11-12lb Beef Brisket
  • Spice Rub:
    • 1/2 cup Brown Sugar
    • 1/4 cup Paprika
    • 1 tbs Salt
    • 1 tbs Pepper
    • 1 tbs Garlic Powder
    • 1 tbs Onion Powder
    • 1 tsp Mustard Powder
  • Charcoal
  • Hickory Wood Chunks
  • Water
  • Butcher Paper
II. Preparation Instructions
  1. Add spice rub ingredients in a bowl and whisk together until combined.
  2. Trim brisket (See video: Trimming Video):
    1. Trim fat until 1/4 inch of fat remains on top.
    2. Trim around edges until curved and smooth.
  3. Pat brisket dry with paper towels.
  4. Cover brisket liberally with spice rub.
  5. Place on a baking sheet with a baking rack. Place in the fridge overnight or until ready to use.
III. Cooking Instructions
  1. Light charcoal in chimney starter: use a wad of paper and cover with neutral cooking oil. Light with a lighter. Place under the chimney starter and fill starter with charcoal (You will need to do this several times throughout the cooking process).
  2. Place a pan filled ~halfway with water on top of the fuel grate on one side.
  3. Once charcoal is white hot, dump charcoal onto the other side of the fuel grate.
  4. Place a grill grate above the fuel grate. Place an ambient temperature probe on the grill grate above the water pan.
  5. Place the grill lid with the air vent side above the water pan.
  6. Allow the grill to preheat to 275 - 300 degrees F.
  7. Open grill, place brisket over water pan.
  8. Let cook for 6-7 hours, or until the internal temperature is at 165-180 degrees F.
  9. Remove brisket, wrap tightly in butcher paper.
  10. Place brisket back on grill for 2-4 hours, or until internal temperature of both flat and point of brisket is at 200-205 degrees F and temperature probe penetrates meat like room temperature butter.
  11. Remove brisket from gril
  12. Place in low temp oven for at least 2 hours and until ready to use.
  13. Slice and enjoy!
